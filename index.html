About this website

On this website, I present my work across several fields, including GitHub repositories, ongoing and planned personal projects, and scientific research in theoretical physics.

Areas of interest

My primary interests lie in theoretical atomic physics, (analog) quantum simulation of molecular processes using Rydberg atoms and ions, electronics, electrodynamics, and and classical mechanics. I am also passionate about computer programming, particularly in C and FORTRAN. I advocate for minimalist, open-source software that provides users with transparency and full control.

Software philosophy

In my personal projects, I focus on building streamlined applications that avoid unnecessary overhead. My philosophy is ‘hands-on’, prioritising performance, maximal control, and minimal third-party dependencies over abstract user-friendliness. This approach ensures that the core functionality remains accessible and efficient.
